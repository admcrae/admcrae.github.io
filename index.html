I am a Ph.D. student in the School of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Georgia Tech, advised by Mark Davenport.
My research is in learning theory and high-dimensional statistics. Most of my work falls under the broad umbrella of understanding how problem structure affects

  1. how many measurements/samples we need to make useful predictions or inferences, and
  2. how much error/corruption we can expect due to noise or other factors.

Here is a non-comprehensive list of topics I've worked on recently:

  • Low-rank (noisy) matrix completion via convex optimization
  • Convex optimization for nonlinear recovery via lifting
    • Reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS) methods
    • (Sparse) phase retrieval
    • (Sparse) principal component analysis (PCA)
  • Interpolation with noise (a.k.a. "Benign overfitting" or "Harmless interpolation")
  • Classification theory (and how it differs from regression)
  • Regression on a manifold domain
